# Patterns: Self-esteem

## Purpose

Recurring patterns in how I evaluate myself: the conditions under which self-worth wobbles, the inner narratives that recur, the events that lift or sink it, and the relationship between self-evaluation and behavior. This file is **especially prone to drift into rumination** if treated as a place to log every bad day — it is not. It is a place to map the *shapes* of self-evaluation that repeat.

## Update rules

- A pattern is added only after **at least 3 distinct observations on different days**.
- Distinguish:
  - **Trigger-linked dips** — self-esteem drops reliably after specific events (a perceived rejection, a missed deliverable, a comparison).
  - **Baseline patterns** — how self-evaluation looks when nothing in particular has happened.
  - **Compensatory behaviors** — what I do *because of* a self-esteem dip (over-working, over-apologizing, withdrawing, performing).
- **No instance-logging.** A single day of low self-esteem belongs in `journal/`, not here. Patterns are recurrences.
- **No interpretation as fact.** "I have low self-esteem because of X in childhood" is a hypothesis, not a pattern. Note it as an open question if it recurs.
- Watch for **self-criticism as a pattern in itself** — the loop, not just the content.

## Entry template

```markdown
### <Pattern name>

- **Type:** trigger-linked / baseline / compensatory
- **Trigger (if any):** <what reliably precedes the dip or shift>
- **Inner content:** <the recurring narrative or comparison — paraphrased, not transcribed>
- **Behavior it produces:** <what I do as a result — observable>
- **Time course:** <how quickly it lifts, what lifts it>
- **Evidence:** `sessions/...`, `journal/...`
- **Counter-evidence:** <times the trigger occurred but the dip did not — what was different>
- **Confidence:** low / medium / high
- **Linked open questions:** <`core/open-questions.md`>
- **Last reviewed:** YYYY-MM-DD
```

## Example

```markdown
### Worth-tied-to-output

- **Type:** trigger-linked
- **Trigger:** Days with no visible deliverable produced — regardless of whether real work was done.
- **Inner content:** "Didn't earn the day." Comparison to a vague more-productive version of self.
- **Behavior it produces:** Reluctance to rest in the evening. Sometimes overworks the next morning to "make up." Sometimes withdraws from partner conversation.
- **Time course:** Resolves overnight if the next day has a visible output. Lingers across multiple low-output days into a heavier mood.
- **Evidence:** `sessions/2026-04-30-no-output-day.md`, `sessions/2026-05-21-no-output-day.md`, `journal/2026-06-03.md`
- **Counter-evidence:** Did not occur on 2026-05-08 — was a low-output day but spent time with friends. Suggests social engagement substitutes for output.
- **Confidence:** medium
- **Linked open questions:** `core/open-questions.md#worth-vs-output`
- **Last reviewed:** 2026-06-09
```
