# Patterns: Relationships

## Purpose

Recurring patterns in how I show up in close relationships — partner, family, friends, and any specific recurring dynamics. Patterns are **analytical artifacts** supporting `psychological-map.md` (Relationships Model). The aim is to map the **shapes** that repeat across people: the same defensive move, the same withdrawal trigger, the same misread of someone's intent.

## Update rules

- A pattern is added only after **at least 3 distinct observations across at least two relationships, or across multiple distinct incidents within one relationship**.
- Every entry has the five required fields: **description, evidence, counter-evidence, confidence, experiments.**
- Names of people: use first initial only, or a stable pseudonym. Be consistent across files.
- **Both sides** of a recurring dynamic must be characterized when possible: my move, the other person's move, the loop it creates.
- Distinguish:
  - **my recurring move** (something I do across relationships)
  - **dyad-specific dynamic** (a loop that exists with one specific person)
  - **context pattern** (how I am in a given setting, e.g., groups vs. 1:1)
- Counter-evidence is especially important here. "I always shut down" is almost never literally true; the question is *when* and *with whom*.
- Privacy: do not transcribe what others said verbatim. Summarize.
- Patterns may *propose* updates to `psychological-map.md` but never replace it.

## Entry template

```markdown
### <Pattern name>

- **Description:** <plain-language description: type (my move / dyad / context), trigger, my move, their move if relevant, the loop, what I notice afterward>
- **Evidence:** `sessions/...`
- **Counter-evidence:** <instances where the trigger occurred and the pattern did NOT — what was different>
- **Confidence:** low / medium / high
- **Experiments:** `experiments/...` (or "proposed: <one-line idea>")
- **Map status:** not promoted / proposed for map / present in map (`section`)
- **Last reviewed:** YYYY-MM-DD
```

## Example

```markdown
### Defensive interpretation of partner feedback

- **Description:** Dyad-specific (with M). When M describes something I did that affected her without first softening the framing, my first reaction is a counter-explanation: why my behavior was reasonable, what I meant. Not an apology, not a question. M reads the counter-explanation as dismissal; tone hardens. Resolution usually requires an hour-plus pause. Jaw tension during; embarrassment when re-reading what was said.
- **Evidence:** `sessions/2026-04-22-partner-feedback.md`, `sessions/2026-05-15-partner-feedback.md`, `sessions/2026-06-05-partner-feedback-loop.md`
- **Counter-evidence:** Did not happen on 2026-05-30 — M started with "I want to tell you something and I'm not upset." Framing reduced perceived threat.
- **Confidence:** medium-high
- **Experiments:** proposed: 2-week practice of asking one clarifying question before responding to any feedback from M.
- **Map status:** proposed for map (Relationships Model)
- **Last reviewed:** 2026-06-09
```
