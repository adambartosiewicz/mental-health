# Patterns: Relationships

## Purpose

Recurring patterns in how I show up in close relationships — partner, family, friends, and any specific recurring dynamics. The aim is not to catalog every conflict but to map the **shapes** that repeat across different people: the same defensive move, the same withdrawal trigger, the same misread of someone's intent.

## Update rules

- A pattern is added only after **at least 3 distinct observations across at least two relationships, or across multiple distinct incidents within one relationship**.
- Names of people: use first initial only, or a stable pseudonym. Be consistent across files.
- **Both sides** of a recurring dynamic must be characterized when possible: my move, the other person's move, the loop it creates.
- Distinguish:
  - **My recurring move** (something I do across relationships)
  - **Dyad-specific dynamic** (a loop that exists with one specific person)
  - **Context pattern** (how I am in a given setting, e.g., groups vs. 1:1)
- Counter-evidence is critical here. "I always shut down" is almost never literally true; the question is *when* and *with whom*.
- Privacy: do not transcribe what others said verbatim. Summarize.

## Entry template

```markdown
### <Pattern name>

- **Type:** my move / dyad-specific / context pattern
- **Trigger:** <what reliably precedes it>
- **My move:** <what I do — observable behavior, not interpretation>
- **Their move (if dyad-specific):** <what the other person reliably does>
- **The loop:** <how the exchange escalates or collapses>
- **What I have noticed afterward:** <body, mood, thought>
- **Evidence:** `sessions/...`
- **Counter-evidence:** <instances where the trigger occurred and the pattern did NOT — what was different>
- **Confidence:** low / medium / high
- **Last reviewed:** YYYY-MM-DD
```

## Example

```markdown
### Defensive interpretation of partner feedback

- **Type:** dyad-specific (with M)
- **Trigger:** M describes something I did that affected her, without first softening the framing.
- **My move:** First reaction is a counter-explanation: why my behavior was reasonable, what I meant. Not an apology, not a question.
- **Their move:** M reads the counter-explanation as dismissal; tone hardens.
- **The loop:** Each side feels increasingly unheard. Resolution usually requires a pause of at least an hour.
- **What I have noticed afterward:** Jaw tension during the exchange. Later, embarrassment when re-reading what was said.
- **Evidence:** `sessions/2026-04-22-partner-feedback.md`, `sessions/2026-05-15-partner-feedback.md`, `sessions/2026-06-05-partner-feedback-loop.md`
- **Counter-evidence:** Did not happen on 2026-05-30 — M started with "I want to tell you something and I'm not upset". Difference: framing reduced perceived threat.
- **Confidence:** medium-high
- **Last reviewed:** 2026-06-09
```
